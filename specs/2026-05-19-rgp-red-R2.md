## 2. Refuted / Partially-Refuted

- **B1-4 recon claim "silences EVERYTHING" тАФ PARTIALLY REFUTED.** The
  `*` learned-allow row does **not** override a builtin **blacklist**:
  blacklist is evaluated before whitelist (`policy/mod.rs:134-157`), so
  `rm -rf /`, `curl|bash`, etc. still hard-Deny at L0. The *real*
  (still HIGH) impact is narrower-but-severe: every L0-**unknown**
  command тАФ the dominant attack class тАФ becomes a hard L0 `Allow` and
  **L1 is never consulted**. The PoC asserts both the bypass (`d2`,`d3`)
  and the refutation (`d4` builtin deny survives). Severity unchanged
  (HIGH/Act): an attacker simply uses a non-blacklisted form (cf. B1-1
  evasion forms in R1's cluster) and L1 is gone.
- **B6-1 `is_available()` probe does NOT itself surface the body.**
  `azure.rs:316-325` `is_available` returns only `bool`; the
  `health.rs` "did not respond" string is generic. The confirmed B6-1
  leak is via (a) `LlmError::Display` тЖТ `policy/llm.rs:162` `warn!`, and
  (b) the **raw `endpoint` printed at `health.rs:91`/`:694`** and
  `create_llm_client_by_name` `Err(e).to_string()` at `health.rs:102`.
  The finding stands; only the specific sink attribution is corrected.
- **No finding refuted to NOT-A-DEFECT** in the R2 cluster. B3-5 and
  B1-3 are confirmed but correctly **in-model / Track** (not blockers).

Nothing on recon's "do NOT re-prove / already mitigated" list was
re-tested (`SecretString` zeroize/Debug, MCP param bounds,
`get_project_rules` home boundary, `validate_key` allowlist, zero-byte
guard, atomic 0600 writes, SSRF host-suffix allowlist).
